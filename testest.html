Catalogue of concerns for plant health in space-based horticulture systems

Willkommen

This tool was developed as part of a Master's thesis titled A Multidisciplinary Framework for Managing Plant Health in Extraterrestrial Horticulture by Saskia Weser at University of applied sciences in Dresden, Germany in cooperation with the German Aerospace Center, Institue of Space Systems, Department Systems Analysis Space Segment (SRS) in Bremen, Germany.

The primary goal of this catalog is to promote and safeguard plant health in space-based cultivation systems by providing a structured overview of potential hazards, underlying mechanisms, and mitigation strategies. It aims to enable mission planners and system designers to prevent failures, enhance system reliability, and maintain functional plant performance under extraterrestrial conditions.

In this context, plant health is defined as the condition that enables plants to fulfill their intended roles within a mission, such as food production, oxygen generation, waste recycling or psychological support for crew members.

Key indicators of plant health include:

  • Consistent growth
  • Yield performance
  • Physiological quality
  • Absence of disease
  • Environmental resilience

This catalog is designed to support:

  • Structured risk assessment in controlled horticultural environments
  • The design of robust and mission-adapted cultivation systems
  • Training of crew members and system operators
  • Resource planning and estimation of essential life support contributions
  • Onboard diagnosis and troubleshooting by astronauts during missions

The catalog is organized in a hierarchical structure to reflect different specificity levels. Each level contributes to a detailed and modular understanding of plant health concerns in controlled or extraterrestrial environments.

Hazard → A broad category of risk that can impact plant health (e.g., Altered Gravity, Improper Environmental Control)
└── Subhazard → A specific type or source of risk within a hazard (e.g., Improper light exposure, Inadequate handling)
    └── Concern → A documented or theoretical issue that affects plant health
        ├── Concern ID → Unique identifier that encodes catalog location
        ├── Affected System Component → Where the issue occurs or impacts
        ├── Description of the Concern → What the issue looks like in practice
        ├── Underlying Risk Mechanism → Biological/technical cause-and-effect link
        ├── Source of the Concern → Based on literature, inference, or observation
        ├── Preventive Management Strategy → How to prevent the issue
        └── Intervention Strategy → How to correct the issue when it occurs

This structure ensures that every potential plant health issue is captured in a consistent, evidence-informed, and actionable way.

Hazard 1: Horticultural Management
Subhazard 1.1: Improper environmental management
Improper light exposure
Concern ID: HM-001 — No bloom development or unwanted bloom
  • Affected System Component: Lighting system
  • Description of the Concern: Flowering anomalies in fruit or leafy crops due to suboptimal photoperiod or light spectrum.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Photoperiodic or spectral stress disrupting hormonal balance and developmental signaling.
  • Source of the Concern: Experienced in EDEN laboratory from german aerospace center
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Provide species-specific light settings; integrate programmable spectrum and timing control.
  • Intervention Strategy: Adjust crop-specific light settings, remove malformed blooms; replant if no improvement.
Concern ID: HM-002 — Intumescence
  • Affected System Component: Lighting and atmosphere control (UV filtering)
  • Description of the Concern: Swelling of plant tissue due to lack of adequate UV radiation.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Cellular dysregulation due to absence of UV-induced physiological checks.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented (Langhans & Tibbits, 1997)
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use UV-transmitting materials; avoid high-pressure sodium lamps.
  • Intervention Strategy: Switch lighting source; reselect varieties with low susceptibility.
Concern ID: HM-003 — Leaf scorch or burns
  • Affected System Component: Lighting system
  • Description of the Concern: Damage to leaf tissue due to excessive light intensity.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Photothermal injury exceeding plant tolerance thresholds.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Design species-appropriate light maps and spatial configuration.
  • Intervention Strategy: Reduce light intensity through software or repositioning of lamps.
Concern ID: HM-004 — Chlorosis and stunted growth
  • Affected System Component: Lighting system
  • Description of the Concern: Pale leaves and slow development due to incorrect spectrum or excessive irradiance, especially after transitioning from low to high light environments.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Impaired chlorophyll synthesis and light-induced metabolic imbalance.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented (Langhans & Tibbits, 1997)
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Provide species-specific lighting; avoid continuous lighting and static temperature regimes.
  • Intervention Strategy: Adjust light intensity and spectrum to optimal values.
Concern ID: HM-005 — Reduced leaf enlargement and stem elongation
  • Affected System Component: Lighting control system
  • Description of the Concern: Inhibited growth dynamics due to insufficient photoperiods.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Short light/dark cycles disrupt photoperiodic signaling and gibberellin pathways.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented (Langhans & Tibbits, 1997)
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Avoid light/dark cycles shorter than 24 hours.
  • Intervention Strategy: Reprogram photoperiod to biologically appropriate cycles.
Concern ID: HM-006 — Increased lignification of stems
  • Affected System Component: Lighting spectrum control
  • Description of the Concern: Excessively rigid stems caused by high blue or UV light exposure.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Stimulation of phenolic metabolism and lignin biosynthesis pathways.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred (from analog growth chamber studies)
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Optimize spectral balance and tune blue/UV intensity per species.
  • Intervention Strategy: Reduce blue/UV light levels dynamically.
Concern ID: HM-007 — Premature leaf senescence
  • Affected System Component: Light quality management system
  • Description of the Concern: Early aging and leaf loss due to low red/far-red ratio (simulating shade conditions).
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Phytochrome-mediated stress signaling induces senescence pathways.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Ensure species-specific light ratios and avoid canopy shading.
  • Intervention Strategy: Adjust light composition to increase red/far-red ratio.
Concern ID: HM-008 — Growth anomalies due to circadian desynchronization
  • Affected System Component: Lighting schedule controller
  • Description of the Concern: Abnormal growth patterns resulting from disruption of circadian rhythms.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Desynchronized internal plant clocks interfere with hormone regulation and metabolism.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred but indicated by Ruts et al.
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Design lighting regimes that match natural day/night cycles or established plant chronotypes.
  • Intervention Strategy: Reset photoperiod synchronization and monitor response.
Concern ID: HM-009 — Algae growth in nutrient solution
  • Affected System Component: Nutrient delivery and lighting exposure interface
  • Description of the Concern: Proliferation of algae in light-exposed nutrient reservoirs with stagnant flow.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Photosynthetic contamination competing for nutrients and oxygen.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented (controlled environment agriculture studies)
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Opaque covers and light shielding; maintain fluid motion.
  • Intervention Strategy: Flush irrigation systems and remove biofilms.
Improper temperatures
Concern ID: HM-010 — Metabolic slowdown
  • Affected System Component: Thermal control system
  • Description of the Concern: Decrease in physiological activity due to cold-induced nutrient uptake limitations.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Reduced enzymatic function and slowed metabolic transport at suboptimal temperatures.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Maintain temperatures above species-specific critical thresholds using environmental controls.
  • Intervention Strategy: Adjust thermal settings promptly and monitor nutrient assimilation rates.
Concern ID: HM-011 — Leaf burns and tissue damage
  • Affected System Component: Lighting and heat emission interface
  • Description of the Concern: Localized overheating leads to physical tissue damage and necrosis.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Thermal stress exceeding tolerance limits, especially near high-output lighting systems.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Ensure sufficient spacing between lamps and plant tissues; use heat shielding.
  • Intervention Strategy: Reduce lighting intensity, increase humidity to buffer temperature peaks.
Concern ID: HM-012 — Flower abortion
  • Affected System Component: Temperature regulation system
  • Description of the Concern: Aborted floral development due to temperature fluctuations beyond reproductive tolerance range.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Heat or cold shock during critical phases of floral induction and pollination.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Stabilize temperature within narrow optimal range during reproductive phases.
  • Intervention Strategy: Restore environmental stability and monitor for compensatory flowering.
Improper atmosphere composition
Concern ID: HM-019 — Ethylene accumulation
  • Affected System Component: Atmosphere management system
  • Description of the Concern: Elevated ethylene levels trigger premature senescence, flower abortion, or growth inhibition.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Ethylene-sensitive signaling pathways alter plant hormonal balance and tissue development.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented (Wheeler 1995; Abeles et al. 1992)
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Integrate ethylene scrubbers (permanganate filters, catalytic burners) and monitor gas composition regularly.
  • Intervention Strategy: Apply ethylene mitigation methods and remove or isolate affected tissues.
Concern ID: HM-020 — Pathogen susceptibility due to ethylene stress
  • Affected System Component: Air composition control
  • Description of the Concern: Ethylene accumulation weakens plant tissues, making them more prone to pathogen attack.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Ethylene-induced cell wall softening reduces resistance to microbial penetration.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Maintain ethylene below threshold levels; enhance circulation and filtration.
  • Intervention Strategy: Diagnose pathogen; remove and isolate infected tissue; sanitize air pathways.
Concern ID: HM-021 — Growth reduction from CO₂ deficiency
  • Affected System Component: CO₂ delivery system
  • Description of the Concern: Reduced vegetative and reproductive performance due to low ambient CO₂ concentration.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Limitation of photosynthesis and carbon assimilation processes.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Supply pressurized CO₂ and maintain optimal levels (1000–2000 ppm).
  • Intervention Strategy: Inject CO₂ and adjust ventilation rates as needed.
Concern ID: HM-022 — Physiological inhibition from high CO₂ levels
  • Affected System Component: Human/plant module air interaction
  • Description of the Concern: Excess CO₂ interferes with physiological processes such as transpiration and photosynthesis.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: CO₂ oversaturation disrupts stomatal function and cellular pH balance.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented (Langhans & Tibbitts)
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Minimize crew exposure in plant area; increase air exchange rates.
  • Intervention Strategy: Vent excess CO₂; use personal filtration gear during high emission phases.
Concern ID: HM-023 — Nutrient uptake inhibition due to CO₂-induced pH imbalance
  • Affected System Component: Root zone pH control system
  • Description of the Concern: High CO₂ alters nitrate uptake, raising pH and impeding micronutrient absorption.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Increased alkalinity disrupts solubility of Fe, Mn, and other ions.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Monitor and buffer pH with balanced nutrient formulations.
  • Intervention Strategy: Apply pH-lowering agents when critical thresholds are exceeded.
Concern ID: HM-024 — Chemical stress from air pollutants
  • Affected System Component: Air purification and filtration
  • Description of the Concern: Pollutants such as ozone, VOCs, and sulfur dioxide cause oxidative damage and physiological disorder.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Reactive oxygen species and toxic byproducts impair cellular structures.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use activated carbon and ozone filters; choose resilient plant varieties.
  • Intervention Strategy: Enhance air exchange and provide antioxidant support (e.g. ascorbate, glutathione).
Improper air humidity
Concern ID: HM-013 — Fungal infection due to condensation
  • Affected System Component: Air humidity regulation system
  • Description of the Concern: Development of fungal pathogens triggered by water condensation on plant surfaces.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Surface moisture fosters fungal spore germination and colonization.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Maintain sufficient temperature gradients to avoid condensation.
  • Intervention Strategy: Reduce relative humidity (rH); increase temperature slightly to eliminate dew point conditions.
Concern ID: HM-014 — Fungal infections due to high humidity and poor air circulation
  • Affected System Component: Air circulation and dehumidification unit
  • Description of the Concern: Accumulation of moist air leads to anoxic microclimates promoting fungal growth.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: High relative humidity combined with air stagnation supports pathogenic proliferation.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Optimize airflow across plant surfaces and regulate humidity under 90%.
  • Intervention Strategy: Enhance air circulation; activate drying cycles or deploy antifungal treatments if needed.
Concern ID: HM-015 — Wilting from low humidity
  • Affected System Component: Humidification and irrigation systems
  • Description of the Concern: Excessive transpiration in dry air causes cellular dehydration and turgor loss.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Low rH increases transpiration beyond water uptake capacity.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Maintain minimum rH above 30% and synchronize with irrigation cycles.
  • Intervention Strategy: Increase ambient humidity; adjust irrigation intervals.
Concern ID: HM-016 — Tipburn due to impaired calcium transport
  • Affected System Component: Atmospheric and nutrient transport interaction
  • Description of the Concern: Nutritional deficiency localized at leaf tips due to environmental imbalance.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Reduced transpiration under high humidity lowers calcium mobility.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented (Tibbitts & Langhans)
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Regulate humidity by day and enhance transpiration cues.
  • Intervention Strategy: Adjust humidity dynamics across light/dark cycle; ensure nutrient uptake efficiency.
Concern ID: HM-017 — Blossom-end rot
  • Affected System Component: Nutrient uptake and transpiration balance
  • Description of the Concern: Localized necrosis on fruits due to disrupted calcium allocation.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Poor transpiration flow prevents calcium reaching fruit extremities.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Maintain balanced transpiration via controlled humidity.
  • Intervention Strategy: Similar to tipburn: optimize humidity and ensure calcium availability.
Concern ID: HM-018 — Electrostatic discharge (ESD) damage
  • Affected System Component: Environmental control and containment
  • Description of the Concern: Physical damage from electrostatic buildup in extremely dry air, especially in small or enclosed growth units.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Dry air and friction generate static electricity that discharges across sensitive plant tissues.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred (Microcool case reports)
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Maintain rH between 40–60% to prevent charge accumulation.
  • Intervention Strategy: Restore optimal humidity; consider use of antistatic materials or ionizing equipment.
Subhazard 1.2: Inadequate handling
Incorrect pruning
Concern ID: HM-025 — Sink-source imbalances
  • Affected System Component: Canopy structure and growth protocol
  • Description of the Concern: Biomass allocation and resilience disrupted by pruning mismanagement.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Disproportionate sink/source ratio disturbs photosynthate distribution.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Provide pruning guidelines based on plant-specific sink/source dynamics.
  • Intervention Strategy: Restore balance by targeted pruning or regrowth stimulation.
Concern ID: HM-026 — Delayed flowering or fruit development
  • Affected System Component: Shoot management protocol
  • Description of the Concern: Improper pruning results in developmental delays.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Energy and hormone distribution altered due to over/under-pruning.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Time pruning to align with plant phenological stages.
  • Intervention Strategy: Adjust light/nutrient balance to stimulate compensatory growth.
Concern ID: HM-027 — Pathogen infection via contaminated tools
  • Affected System Component: Crew tool handling protocol
  • Description of the Concern: Improper sanitation of cutting tools leads to infection risk.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Pathogens introduced via wounds under favorable conditions.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use sterilized sharp tools and operate in controlled environments.
  • Intervention Strategy: Diagnose and isolate infection sites; sanitize tools and restore environmental control.
Improper pollination
Concern ID: HM-028 — No or reduced fruit set
  • Affected System Component: Reproduction management system
  • Description of the Concern: Failure of fruit development due to inefficient pollination.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Incomplete pollen transfer and fertilization under suboptimal conditions.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Employ parthenocarpic species or apply manual pollination under optimal conditions.
  • Intervention Strategy: Adjust microclimate; manually transfer pollen; reassess varietal choice.
Concern ID: HM-029 — Misshaped fruit due to incomplete fertilization
  • Affected System Component: Fertility management
  • Description of the Concern: Malformed fruit as a result of poor pollination.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Partial fertilization disrupts hormonal cues for balanced growth.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Ensure environmental control during pollination phase.
  • Intervention Strategy: Optimize temperature and humidity; repeat pollination cycle.
Concern ID: HM-030 — Seedless fruit in generative systems
  • Affected System Component: Reproductive cycle control
  • Description of the Concern: Parthenocarpy prevents seed development in long-term systems relying on propagation.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Lack of fertilization in parthenocarpic cultivars blocks seed formation.
  • Source of the Concern: Theoretical
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use open-pollinated, non-hybrid cultivars for long-term sustainability.
  • Intervention Strategy: Ensure functioning manual or airflow-assisted pollination system.
Mechanical damage
Concern ID: HM-031 — Broken plant parts
  • Affected System Component: Physical interaction with crew or robotics
  • Description of the Concern: Structural damage to stems, leaves, or roots during handling or maintenance.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Mechanical stress causes open wounds and potential infection entry points.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Establish handling protocols with spatial guidelines and support structures.
  • Intervention Strategy: Remove damaged parts; apply wound care and monitor infection risk.
Concern ID: HM-032 — Root damage during transplanting
  • Affected System Component: Transplantation tools and techniques
  • Description of the Concern: Root systems become compromised during manual or automated relocation.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Mechanical tearing or desiccation interrupts water and nutrient uptake.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use minimally invasive tools; maintain root moisture and limit exposure time.
  • Intervention Strategy: Optimize post-transplant humidity and provide recovery buffer nutrients.
Concern ID: HM-033 — Reduced photosynthetic efficiency due to crushed leaves
  • Affected System Component: Spatial configuration of growth chamber
  • Description of the Concern: Compaction or overlapping of leaves reduces light capture and gas exchange.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Damaged or shaded tissue diminishes carbon assimilation rates.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Design leaf spacing systems and monitor plant architecture.
  • Intervention Strategy: Separate or prune overlapping leaves; stimulate new canopy development.
Subhazard 1.3: Irrigation/Nutrition
Watering inconsistencies
Concern ID: HM-034 — Hypoxia in root zone
  • Affected System Component: Irrigation system design
  • Description of the Concern: Water accumulation in root area, especially in microgravity.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Oxygen deprivation to roots, inhibiting nutrient uptake.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use gravity-independent watering methods and drainage design (e.g., capillary mats).
  • Intervention Strategy: Improve water flow and remove excess water manually or via system reprogramming.
Concern ID: HM-035 — Fruit cracking
  • Affected System Component: Irrigation and nutrient timing control
  • Description of the Concern: Sudden water uptake causes ruptures in fruit skin.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Elasticity threshold of skin exceeded due to osmotic imbalance.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented (Langhans, Tibbitts & Peet, 1992)
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Maintain consistent irrigation schedule and support calcium balance.
  • Intervention Strategy: Use calcium sprays or gibberellic acid to strengthen fruit tissue.
Concern ID: HM-036 — Wilting due to insufficient watering
  • Affected System Component: Water delivery system
  • Description of the Concern: Lack of water leads to turgor loss and cellular collapse.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Transpiration exceeds uptake, reducing internal pressure.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Automate irrigation based on soil moisture sensors.
  • Intervention Strategy: Increase water volume or frequency until recovery observed.
Concern ID: HM-037 — Disruptions in nutrient uptake
  • Affected System Component: Nutrient flow dynamics
  • Description of the Concern: Nutrient transport is impaired by inconsistent hydration patterns.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Fluctuating water levels lead to erratic ion transport in roots.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Match irrigation schedules with transpiration curves.
  • Intervention Strategy: Normalize hydration profile and replenish lost nutrients.
Improper nutrient supply
Concern ID: HM-038 — Nutrient deficiencies
  • Affected System Component: Nutrient formulation
  • Description of the Concern: Essential elements missing or imbalanced, causing visible plant stress.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Impaired metabolic pathways, chlorosis, necrosis or malformations.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Calibrate nutrient solutions based on plant species and growth phase.
  • Intervention Strategy: Apply targeted supplementation based on deficiency diagnosis.
Concern ID: HM-039 — Nutrient toxicities
  • Affected System Component: Nutrient concentration management
  • Description of the Concern: Oversupply of certain nutrients causes toxic buildup.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Osmotic stress, ion competition, or pH imbalances.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Monitor EC levels and balance nutrient input.
  • Intervention Strategy: Dilute nutrient solution or flush system with purified water.
pH imbalances
Concern ID: HM-040 — Disruption of nutrient uptake due to pH
  • Affected System Component: pH regulation and sensing
  • Description of the Concern: Improper pH impairs nutrient solubility and root function.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Altered ionic state and reduced availability of nutrients.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use pH sensors and buffers; maintain target range.
  • Intervention Strategy: Adjust pH with acid/base corrections.
Salinity and osmotic stress
Concern ID: HM-041 — Osmotic stress and ion toxicity
  • Affected System Component: EC control and nutrient salinity
  • Description of the Concern: High salt levels cause reduced water uptake and cellular dehydration.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Osmotic pressure gradient prevents water absorption and damages roots.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Monitor EC; balance ion concentrations.
  • Intervention Strategy: Dilute solution with reverse osmosis water and increase drainage.
Hazard 2: Hostile/Closed Environment
Subhazard 2.1: Improper resource management
Concern ID: HCE-001 — Shortage of critical operation materials
  • Affected System Component: Material supply chain
  • Description of the Concern: Shortages of fertilizers, substrates, spare parts, etc..
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: False calculations, unseen needs or malfunctioning recycling loops.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use of precise calculation models, maintain backup stock.
  • Intervention Strategy: Redistribution if possible, initiate rationing or emergency sourcing protocols.
Concern ID: HCE-002 — Waste/gas-related phytotoxicity
  • Affected System Component: Waste management and nutrient delivery system
  • Description of the Concern: Root zone and plant tissue damage due to toxic gases like ammonia or hydrogen sulfide.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Decomposition gases from organic waste.
  • Source of the Concern: Referenced in H1 atmosphere section
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Strict hygiene: immediate waste removal, ensure proper aeration.
  • Intervention Strategy: Flush system, improve oxygenation, replace solution.
Concern ID: HCE-003 — Microbial proliferation from harvest waste
  • Affected System Component: Waste and sanitation system
  • Description of the Concern: Accumulated organic matter promotes pathogenic microbial growth.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Microbial buildup due to unmanaged waste.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Strict hygiene: immediate waste removal, ensure proper aeration.
  • Intervention Strategy: if infection occurs, Diagnose pathogen, treat and dispose infected tissue, ensure circulation.
Subhazard 2.2: Regolith and dust contamination
Concern ID: HCE-004 — Dysfunctinal plant metabolism and microbial life
  • Affected System Component: Root zone
  • Description of the Concern: Contamination with toxic perchlorates or unknown compounds.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Toxicity from Martian regolith (e.g. perchlorates, heavy metals).
  • Source of the Concern: Documented (Wang; Misra; Hall)
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use habitat airlocks, remove perchlorates via biocatalysis/filtration, regular cleaning.
  • Intervention Strategy: Ongoing research – implement current best practices where possible.
Concern ID: HCE-005 — Reduced photosynthesis
  • Affected System Component: Plant surfaces and stomata
  • Description of the Concern: Dust covering leaves blocks light and gas exchange.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Surface coverage and optical interference.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Regular cleaning, air filtration, design to prevent dust accumulation.
  • Intervention Strategy: Manual or automated plant surface cleaning.
Concern ID: HCE-006 — Hardware/system malfunction due to "normal" dust
  • Affected System Component: Subsystem filters and moving parts (fans, pumps, robotics)
  • Description of the Concern:Mechanical failure from dust clogging or abrasion.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Dust particles entering moving components and filtration.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use dust-sealed hardware, modular replaceable filters, active air filtration.
  • Intervention Strategy: Manual or automated hardware surface cleaning.
Subhazard 2.3: Material decay in long-term missions
Concern ID: HCE-007 — Microplastic contamination of water cycles and plants itself
  • Affected System Component: Irrigation system, plants
  • Description of the Concern: Plastics degrade over time, contaminating water with particles.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Aging of synthetic materials under mission conditions.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use non-plastic components (PTFE, stainless steel), particle monitoring.
  • Intervention Strategy: Physical filtration, part replacement, material upgrades.
Concern ID: HCE-008 — Leaks in pipes and mechanical connections
  • Affected System Component: Irrigation system
  • Description of the Concern: Moisture loss, pressure loss.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Material fatigue from pressure, vibration, thermal cycling.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use fatigue-resistant materials, simulate stress before mission, install leak sensors.
  • Intervention Strategy: Isolate defective parts, patch with epoxy/self-healing polymers, use 3D-printed spares.
Concern ID: HCE-009 — Hydroponic system failure
  • Affected System Component: Irrigation system
  • Description of the Concern: Corrosion-induced system damage.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Accelerated corrosion from radiation and moisture.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use corrosion-resistant materials and radiation shielding.
  • Intervention Strategy: Isolate defective parts, patch with epoxy/self-healing polymers, use 3D-printed spares.
Concern ID: HCE-010 — Gradual performance loss of systems (for example lighting)
  • Affected System Component: CEA subsystems and sensors
  • Description of the Concern: LEDs and sensitive components degrade slowly over time.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Thermal cycling, dust, and wear on electronic systems.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Use industrial-grade, modular LEDs, protect from dust, monitor output.
  • Intervention Strategy: Replace panels, clean optics, if needed fabricate with 3D printing.
Subhazard 2.4: Pest entry and uncontrolled spread
Concern ID: HCE-011 — Cross-contamination from other modules
  • Affected System Component: Biocontainment and sanitation systems, humans, plants

Hazard 3: Altered Gravity
Subhazard 3.1: Irrigation
Concern ID: AG-001 — Hypoxia in roots and epinastic leaves
  • Affected System Component: Root zone
  • Description of the Concern: In microgravity, water tends to cling to root surfaces due to increased surface tension, which blocks oxygen diffusion and causes root hypoxia. Epinastic leaf curvature is also observed as a stress response.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Absence of gravitational drainage leads to water accumulation, suppressing passive gas exchange at the root surface.
  • Source of the Concern: Documented (Levine 2021; APH and PONDS experiments)
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Develop passive or semi-passive irrigation systems adapted to microgravity, e.g., pressure-regulated pumps (APH) or capillary wicking (PONDS).
  • Intervention Strategy: Adjust irrigation protocol; modify root substrate or wick system; monitor oxygenation.
Concern ID: AG-002 — Increased pathogen infection risk
  • Affected System Component: Root, Leaf and stem surfaces
  • Description of the Concern: Without gravity, water droplets stagnate on plant surfaces, creating a moist microenvironment favorable for pathogen growth.
  • Underlying Risk Mechanism: Lack of natural drainage and convection allows persistent water films, promoting microbial colonization.
  • Source of the Concern: Inferred
  • Preventive Management Strategy: Ensure air movement using fans or airflow paths.
  • Intervention Strategy: Manually remove water films; increase airflow; apply antimicrobial protocols if needed.
Concern ID: AG-003 — Non-uniform humidity distribution

  • Description of the Concern: In the absence of gravity-driven convection, water vapor accumulates unevenly, leading to humidity hotspots or dry zones.
